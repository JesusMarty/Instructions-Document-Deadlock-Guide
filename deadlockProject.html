01

Why This Looks Scary
(And Why It Isn't)

Deadlock is three familiar games stacked on top of each other: third-person shooter aim, MOBA lane-and-item economy, and RPG-style character builds. Nobody explains all three at once, so it feels overwhelming in your first match.

You already know pieces of this. If you've played any shooter, you know aim. If you've played any game with a shop, you know buying upgrades. This guide is just the missing connective tissue.

The promise of this document: Souls, camps, objectives, items, characters, and scaling will feel like six small ideas instead of one big scary one.

GOLDEN RULE 01 — You don't need to know all different sections fully, this will help you stand your ground against other people and will guide you into starting.
02

Souls — Currency & Progress

Souls are Deadlock's only currency and its XP. No souls = no leveling up = no items = losing the game. It is the essense of the game and is very important.

  • Souls = they come from troopers that spawn in every 30 seconds, killing players, boxes around the map, minions, and objectives
  • Securing souls = melee kill(punching) a trooper will secure 100% of the soul. Killing it in any other way will drop a floating orb where it can be stolen.
  • Melee kill = instant secured souls, no orb, nothing to lose.
  • Ranged/ability kill = drops a floating orb. Green = yours to confirm. Orange/red = enemy's, denyable.
GOLDEN RULE 02 — Secure your own orb every time. Denying is a bonus skill, not a requirement.
03

Camps — Where Else Souls Come From

Jungle camps sit between lanes. Clearing them gives bonus souls, split evenly among whoever helps. Indicated on the map with a triangle.

  • Tier 1(No line) — small camps, up ~2:00, weakest, safest to solo.
  • Tier 2(One line) — medium camps, up ~6–7:00, better payout, bring a friend.
  • Tier 3(Two lines) — hard camps, up ~8:00, best payout, high risk alone.
GOLDEN RULE 03 — Never clear a camp while your lane wave is dying. The wave is always worth more than the camp.
04

Objectives — Midboss, Urn, Unstable Rift, Guardians

Guardians/Walkers — lane towers, very tanky, very strong, don't attack unless you have troopers. Gaurdians taken by one or two peoples, Walkers need a team of 3 or more.

Midboss — big neutral boss, grants team rejuvinator, healing plus 3 revives, very important, try to steal when enemy are taking it or make sure to secure it when your team is taking it.

Urn — a carried objective dropping it at a specific location gives you souls. Does not need the entire team maybe one or two people, needs the whole team.

Unstable Rift — king of the hill capture point objectice, gives souls, and super troopers that can help you take towers, very important needs the team.

GOLDEN RULE 04 — Never contest an objective you can't see the enemy team for. They can always fight you on it be aware of your map.
05

Builds & Items

Every item falls into one of three lanes. You don't need all three — you need to know which ones your hero wants.

  • Weapon — bullet damage, fire rate, clip size.
  • Vitality — health, regen, survivability.
  • Spirit — ability power, cooldowns, magic damage.
  • Item Tiers — every item is put into different tiers, and buying a previous tier can branch off into better ones.
GOLDEN RULE 05 — You dont need to invest time into building. Fortunately the game has a built in browser to see the best and most popular builds so you can focus on the game and having fun.
06

Characters — Picking Your First Hero

CARRY
damage-focused
Simple kit, high impact once ahead, a lot of farming, high damage, can win games by yourself.
BRUISER / TANK
front-line
High health, simple rotation, near-impossible to feel "useless" while learning, hard to get killed.
SUPPORT
healing / utility
Contributes even during a rough personal game, great for absolute first-timers, always in team fights, can save games.
ASSASSIN / FLEX
high skill ceiling
Save this one for game 10+, mechanically demanding, high risk high reward, if the game feels too easy.
GOLDEN RULE 06 — Pick charaters you find intereseting. Try and pick one from each section, find a play style you like, see which one you have the most fun on.
07

Scaling & Damage

Bullet damage - scales aggressively, reliable early, super dominant late game.

Spirit damage - starts strong and is consistent during early and mid but tends to fall off late game.

Boons - these are your levels, scales everything health, ammo, damage, regen, it all depends on your charater.

GOLDEN RULE 07 — If you are weaking weak during the early game it is ok, you always have time to scale and feel strong. Don't get discourage if your early and mid game are bad.
08

Putting It Together — Your First Game

Before the match: practice heros in the hideout(main menu) and pick which ones you like

0–5 min: last-hit troopers, clear a Tier 1 camp only if your lane is safe, try to get kills.

5–10 min: Buy needed items, take guardians, rotate to lanes that need help.

10–25 min: group for objectives or walkers with vision first, look for rift fights, urn, or if team is all dead go for mid boss.

25+ min: play careful everyone is strong, focus on good picks and have fun.
